## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

This briefing synthesizes current literature (2020–2026) on how macrophage phenotypes and secreted factors govern the transition of dormant disseminated tumor cells (DTCs) to overt metastatic outgrowth. The central finding is that macrophages are active drivers at every stage — from priming DTCs for dormancy before dissemination, to preparing the pre-metastatic niche, to delivering the decisive reactivation signals at distant organs. Therapeutic interventions including surgery, chemotherapy, and radiation can paradoxically shift macrophage polarization toward pro-reawakening phenotypes.

## SECTION 1: REACTIVATION TRIGGERS

### 1.1 The TGFbeta1-NRP2 Axis
Recalde-Percaz et al. (2025, *Neoplasia*, PMID 40848614): Macrophage-derived TGFbeta1 upregulates NRP2 on DTCs → proliferation, invasion, survival. NRP2 deletion forces quiescence via p27, NR2F1, RARB, SOX9.

### 1.2 Fibrotic-Like Niche (FLN) Formation
Gilon-Zaltsman et al. (2025, *Cancer Letters*, PMID 39826669): Pro-resolving macrophages (Mres) prevent FLN establishment by inhibiting fibroblast-to-myofibroblast differentiation and eliminating myofibroblasts via ROS-mediated apoptosis.

### 1.3 Primary Tumor Macrophages Prime DTCs for Dormancy Before Dissemination
Borriello et al. (2022, *Nature Communications*, PMID 35110548): Using WHRIL intravital imaging, demonstrated that macrophage contact induces both pro-dissemination AND pro-dormancy programs (NR2F1+, MenaINV+, SOX9+). Macrophage depletion with clodronate liposomes REDUCES dormancy markers.

### 1.4 Macrophage-Derived MMP9 and LOX
Jenovai (2022, Doctoral Thesis): MMP9 degrades basement membrane constraining DTCs; LOX-mediated collagen crosslinking increases tissue stiffness — a mechanical cue for reactivation.

### 1.5 Extracellular Vesicles and miRNAs
Friedman-DeLuca et al. (2024, *Frontiers in Immunology*, PMID 39555068): TAM-derived EVs carry miRNAs reshaping DTC transcription. IL-6/JAK2/STAT3/miR-34a/SNAIL axis dismantles dormancy programs.

### 1.6 NET-TAM Synergy
He et al. (2026, *J Hematol Oncol*, PMID 41987275) and Tang et al. (2020, *Clin Transl Med*, PMID 32508035): NETs stimulate macrophages via IL-1beta; activated macrophages amplify inflammatory cascades breaking dormancy.

## SECTION 2: PRE-METASTATIC NICHE PREPARATION

### 2.1 TMEM Doorways as Conduits
Duran et al. (2025, *Oncogene*, PMID 40646332): Targeting CSF-1 signaling at TMEM doorways inhibits breast cancer dissemination. Parmar et al. (2026, *NPJ Breast Cancer*, PMID 41495055): TMEM scores associated with distant recurrence across subtypes.

### 2.2 Primed Niches and Dormancy Bypass
Borriello et al. (2022): Macrophage-primed DTCs arrive with dormancy programs pre-activated. Dalla et al. (2024, *Cell*): Lung-resident alveolar macrophages regulate timing of metastasis.

---

## SECTION 3: THERAPY-INDUCED REACTIVATION

### 3.1 Surgical Trauma
Tang et al. (2020, PMID 32508035): Post-surgical DAMPs → NF-kappaB → immunosuppression → TAM expansion → dormant cell awakening. Vulnerability window: ~1 week to 6 months.

### 3.2 Chemotherapy Paradox
Friedman-DeLuca et al. (2024): Chemotherapy can repolarize TAMs toward both anti-tumor AND pro-metastatic phenotypes, depending on patient-specific factors.

### 3.3 Radiation-Induced Polarization
Grant & Ferrer (2026, *Trends Cell Biol*, PMID 40628544): Radiation recruits monocytes that differentiate into TAMs under damaged microenvironment influence.

## SECTION 5: TEMPORAL DYNAMICS

### Pre-Dissemination Phase
Dormancy programming occurs at primary tumor BEFORE dissemination (Borriello et al., 2022).

### Reactivation Window
Convergence of: (1) loss of dormancy-maintaining macrophages, (2) FLN establishment, (3) TGFbeta1 surge, (4) inflammatory perturbation.

### Post-Surgical Vulnerability Window
- Days 0–7: Peak immunosuppression, highest reactivation risk
- Days 7–14: Immune restoration begins
- Weeks–months: Persistent low-grade immunosuppression in vulnerable patients
- Up to 6 months: Subclinical immunosuppressive milieu may persist

## SECTION 6: CLINICAL CORRELATES

### CD68/CD163 Ratio in Breast Cancer
Darvishian et al. (2022): High CD68/CD163 ratio >0.46 predicts DCIS recurrence (HR 10.32).

### TMEM Doorway Scores
Parmar et al. (2026): TMEM scores associated with distant recurrence across breast cancer subtypes.

### Prostate Cancer
Molina et al. (2023, *Front Immunol*, PMID 37435060): CD163+ macrophages predict late adverse outcomes in prostate cancer.

### Thyroid Cancer
Katoh et al. (2024, *J Surg Res*, PMID 39426065): CD163+ TAMs predict papillary thyroid cancer recurrence.

## SUMMARY

1. Macrophages actively install dormancy programs before dissemination (Borriello et al., 2022)
2. Tissue-resident macrophages serve as gatekeepers maintaining dormancy (Dalla et al., 2024)
3. Fibrotic-like niche enabled by absence of pro-resolving macrophages permits outgrowth (Gilon-Zaltsman et al., 2025)
4. TGFbeta1-NRP2 axis links macrophage signals to dormancy exit (Recalde-Percaz et al., 2025)
5. Surgery creates a defined vulnerability window (Tang et al., 2020)
6. Clinical markers (CD68/CD163 ratio, TMEM scores) have quantitative prognostic value
