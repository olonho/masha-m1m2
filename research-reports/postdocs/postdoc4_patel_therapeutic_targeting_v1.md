# RESEARCH BRIEFING: Therapeutic Targeting of Macrophage Polarization to Maintain Dormancy or Prevent Reactivation of Disseminated Tumor Cells

**Prepared by:** Dr. Arjun Patel, PhD
**Date:** May 26, 2026
**Focus Area:** Therapeutic strategies -- targeting macrophage polarization to maintain dormancy or prevent reactivation of DTCs

---

## 1. CSF1R Inhibitors: Current Status in Solid Tumors

### 1.1 Pexidartinib (PLX3397)

Pexidartinib is the most clinically advanced CSF1R inhibitor and remains the only one to receive FDA approval (August 2019) for tenosynovial giant cell tumor (TGCT), a non-malignant neoplasm driven by CSF1 overexpression. Its application in solid malignancies has been extensively studied but with limited success as monotherapy.

Key clinical data in solid tumors:
- A **Phase Ib study** (Wesolowski et al., Ther Adv Med Oncol, 2019; PMID: 31258629) evaluated pexidartinib plus paclitaxel in 24 patients with advanced solid tumors. The combination demonstrated that pexidartinib effectively blocked CSF1R signaling and showed potential for mitigating paclitaxel-induced macrophage infiltration, but objective responses were modest.
- A **Phase I study in Asian patients** (Lee et al., Invest New Drugs, 2020; PMID: 30825104) showed tolerable safety but limited efficacy in advanced solid tumors, with only one patient (TGCT) achieving objective response.
- A **Pediatric Phase I trial** (Boal et al., Clin Cancer Res, 2020; PMID: 32943455) evaluated pexidartinib in refractory solid tumors and leukemias in children. The study demonstrated target engagement (reduced peripheral monocytes) but no significant clinical responses in solid tumors.

Critically, **Omstead et al. (Carcinogenesis, 2022; PMID: 35552655)** demonstrated that pexidartinib sensitized esophageal adenocarcinoma to anti-PD-1 therapy in a rat model, providing preclinical rationale for combining CSF1R inhibition with checkpoint blockade. This is relevant to the dormancy field because the combination may reshape the microenvironment to sustain immune surveillance over dormant DTCs.

### 1.2 Emactuzumab and Cabiralizumab

- **Emactuzumab** (RG7155/RO5509554, Roche): A monoclonal anti-CSF1R antibody. Phase I/IB data showed dramatic reductions in TAMs (especially CD163+ cells) in tumor biopsies, but limited single-agent clinical activity in solid tumors. Combination trials with atezolizumab (anti-PD-L1) have been pursued. The critical gap is that no published data address effects on dormant DTCs or late metastatic recurrence.

- **Cabiralizumab** (FPA-144, Five Prime/Bristol-Myers Squibb): An anti-CSF1R antibody tested in combination with nivolumab. A Phase Ia/IB study in advanced solid tumors (presented at SITC 2019) showed TAM depletion but the combination did not meet efficacy endpoints in a Phase II pancreatic cancer trial. Development appears to have been deprioritized.

### 1.3 Relevance to Dormancy

No CSF1R inhibitor has been specifically tested in the context of dormant DTCs or metastatic recurrence prevention. This represents a significant gap. The theoretical concern is paradoxical: while CSF1R blockade depletes immunosuppressive M2-like TAMs, it also depletes macrophage populations that may be actively maintaining dormancy through TGF-beta and other signaling. The 2025 review by **Zhang et al. (Cancer Metastasis Rev, 2025; PMID: 40762813)** explicitly discusses this, noting that TAMs maintain dormancy through TGF-beta, WNT, and HIPPO pathway modulation, and that distinct macrophage subtypes differentially regulate the dormancy-proliferation switch. Blanket elimination of macrophages could therefore inadvertently remove dormancy-maintaining signals.

## 2. Macrophage Repolarization Agents

### 2.1 PI3K-gamma Inhibitors: Eganelisib (IPI-549)

Eganelisib is the most clinically advanced macrophage repolarization agent and provides the strongest translational data in this space.

**MARIO-1 Trial** (Hong et al., Clin Cancer Res, 2023; PMID: 37000164): This Phase 1/1b first-in-human study (NCT02637531) evaluated eganelisib as monotherapy (n=39) and combined with nivolumab (n=180) in advanced solid tumors. Key findings:
- Orally administered, highly selective PI3K-gamma inhibitor
- Doses of 30-40 mg QD in combination with PD-1/PD-L1 inhibitors were chosen for Phase 2
- Antitumor activity was observed in combination, including in patients who had progressed on prior PD-1/PD-L1 therapy
- Manageable safety profile, with reversible hepatic enzyme elevations as the main toxicity

**MARIO-3 Trial -- Translational Analyses** (O'Connell et al., J Immunother Cancer, 2024; PMID: 39214650): This Phase 2 study (NCT03961698) evaluated eganelisib + atezolizumab + nab-paclitaxel in frontline metastatic TNBC. Paired tumor biopsies demonstrated:
- Gene signatures of TAM reprogramming from immunosuppressive to immune-activating phenotype
- Immune activation regardless of baseline PD-L1 status
- Extracellular matrix (ECM) reorganization, particularly relevant because ECM signals are known regulators of dormancy
- Peripheral blood analyses confirmed systemic immune activation

**Note on clinical development status:** Despite promising translational data, Infinity Pharmaceuticals ceased operations in 2023, and eganelisib's clinical development has been halted. The intellectual property and future development pathway remain uncertain.

### 2.2 STING Agonists

The STING (Stimulator of Interferon Genes) pathway has emerged as a powerful lever for macrophage repolarization. Key recent findings:

- **Yang et al. (Proc Natl Acad Sci USA, 2025; PMID: 41264244)** engineered a "macrophage circuit" (PMMB) integrating a CSF1R inhibitor (BLZ945) and a STING agonist within a macrophage-mimetic nanostructure, enabling sequential TAM reprogramming in pancreatic cancer. This combined depletion-plus-repolarization strategy represents a next-generation approach.

- **Sun et al. (J Immunother Cancer, 2023; PMID: 36927529)** demonstrated that macrophage STING signaling promotes NK cell activation via 4-1BBL/4-1BB co-stimulation to suppress colorectal cancer liver metastasis -- directly relevant to metastatic colonization, a process intimately linked to dormancy exit.

- **Yang et al. (Nat Nanotechnol, 2022; PMID: 36302963)** developed ZnCDA, a tumor-targeted STING agonist nanoparticle (zinc cyclic di-AMP) that achieved tumor-associated macrophage reinvigoration and antitumor activity.

- **Gusliakova et al. (Biomater Adv, 2026; PMID: 41380376)** demonstrated light-directed TAM reprogramming via STING agonist delivery using controllable release systems.

- **Xin et al. (Mater Today Bio, 2026; PMID: 42006712)** developed mannosylated graphene oxide nanotherapeutics co-delivering docetaxel and a STING agonist (DMXAA), demonstrating M2-to-M1 repolarization and antitumor immunity potentiation.

### 2.3 CD40 Agonists

CD40 agonists activate antigen-presenting cells including macrophages, promoting M1-like polarization. The landmark **Beatty et al. (Science, 2015)** study showed that CD40 agonist (CP-870,893) activated macrophages and induced tumor regression in pancreatic cancer patients. However, recent clinical development has been challenging:
- Combination trials with chemotherapy and checkpoint inhibitors in pancreatic cancer and other solid tumors have shown mixed results
- No specific data address effects on dormancy or DTC reactivation
- Toxicity concerns (cytokine release syndrome) have limited dosing

### 2.4 TLR Agonists and HDAC Inhibitors

- **TLR agonists** (TLR7/8, TLR9): These promote M1-like polarization. Topical imiquimod (TLR7) and intratumoral SD-101 (TLR9) have been tested in clinical trials. Wanderley et al. demonstrated that paclitaxel reprograms TAMs to M1 profiles in a TLR4-dependent manner, providing mechanistic rationale for chemo-immunotherapy combinations.
- **HDAC inhibitors**: Vorinostat and other pan-HDAC inhibitors have shown preclinical capacity to modulate macrophage polarization, but clinical data specific to dormancy are lacking.

## 3. Combination Strategies for Preventing Metastatic Relapse

### 3.1 CSF1R Inhibition + Anti-PD-1/PD-L1

The preclinical rationale is strong: CSF1R inhibitors reduce immunosuppressive TAMs, potentially restoring T-cell infiltration and overcoming checkpoint resistance. Omstead et al. (2022; PMID: 35552655) demonstrated this specifically in esophageal adenocarcinoma. However, clinical trials (e.g., cabiralizumab + nivolumab in pancreatic cancer) have not met primary endpoints. The dormancy field requires careful consideration: these combinations may eliminate macrophages that sustain dormancy while simultaneously enhancing immune surveillance. The net effect on DTCs is unpredictable without context-specific data.

### 3.2 PI3K-gamma Inhibition + ICI + Chemotherapy

The MARIO-3 trial (O'Connell et al., 2024; PMID: 39214650) provides the most advanced clinical data for a macrophage-reprogramming triplet combination. The ECM reorganization observed is particularly relevant to dormancy, as ECM stiffness and composition are known dormancy regulators. This combination strategy merits investigation in the adjuvant setting specifically to prevent dormant DTC reactivation.

### 3.3 STING Agonists + CSF1R Inhibition

The Yang et al. (PNAS, 2025; PMID: 41264244) "macrophage circuit" combining sequential CSF1R blockade and STING activation represents a mechanistically sophisticated approach. For dormancy, the strategy could theoretically: (1) deplete immunosuppressive TAMs that promote reactivation, then (2) repolarize residual macrophages to M1-like phenotypes that maintain immune surveillance.

### 3.4 Radiotherapy + Macrophage Modulation

Radiotherapy is known to alter the immune microenvironment and can promote both immune activation (through immunogenic cell death) and immunosuppression (through TAM recruitment). Combining radiotherapy with macrophage-reprogramming agents in the adjuvant setting could be a strategy to prevent metastatic recurrence, though no clinical data specifically address dormancy endpoints.

## 5. Biomarkers for Monitoring Macrophage Polarization States

### 5.1 Single-Cell RNA Sequencing (scRNA-seq) Signatures

The field has been transformed by scRNA-seq, which has revealed that the classical M1/M2 dichotomy is a simplification. Recent key developments:

- **CXCL9/SPP1 macrophage polarity axis**: Bill et al. (referenced in the O'Connell 2024 paper) identified CXCL9:SPP1 ratio as a polarization metric that identifies a network of cellular programs controlling human cancers. Li et al. (Front Immunol, 2026; PMID: 42079623) extended this to NSCLC, identifying four distinct TAM subpopulations through integration of scRNA-seq and bulk transcriptomics.
- scRNA-seq is increasingly used to characterize TAM heterogeneity across cancer types, with >166 published studies identified in our PubMed search (2020-2026).

### 5.2 Spatial Transcriptomics

- Technologies such as GeoMx Digital Spatial Profiling (used in the MARIO-3 trial) allow spatially resolved measurement of macrophage polarization states within tumor biopsies.
- Spatial transcriptomics can identify niches where immunosuppressive TAMs cluster -- potentially corresponding to dormant DTC niches.

### 5.3 Multiplex Immunofluorescence and Imaging Biomarkers

- Multiplex IF panels (e.g., CD68/CD163/CD80/CD86/iNOS) allow simultaneous visualization of macrophage density and polarization state in tissue sections.
- The **AstroPath platform** (Berry et al., Science, 2021) demonstrated that multispectral imaging of macrophage signatures can predict response to PD-1 blockade, validating the concept that macrophage polarization state is a clinically actionable biomarker.

### 5.4 Circulating Biomarkers

- **Peripheral blood monocyte profiling**: Flow cytometry of circulating monocytes (classical vs. non-classical subsets) and soluble CSF1 levels can serve as pharmacodynamic biomarkers for CSF1R inhibitors.
- **Soluble CD163 (sCD163)**: A shedding product of the hemoglobin scavenger receptor, elevated sCD163 correlates with M2-like macrophage activity and poor prognosis in several cancers.
- **Cytokine panels**: Multiplex cytokine analysis of plasma (e.g., IL-10, TGF-beta, CCL22, CXCL9, CXCL10) can provide readouts of macrophage polarization state.

### 5.5 PET Imaging

- Radiolabeled antibodies and small molecules targeting macrophage-specific markers (e.g., (64)Cu-labeled anti-CD206 for M2-like TAMs) are being developed for non-invasive imaging of TAM polarization. These could potentially be deployed to monitor the dormant niche.

## 6. Challenges and Risks

### 6.1 The Dormancy Paradox

This is the central challenge identified in the literature and highlighted by Zhang et al. (2025; PMID: 40762813): **macrophages are not uniformly promoters or suppressors of dormancy.** Distinct macrophage subtypes play opposing roles:

- **Dormancy-maintaining macrophages**: Through secretion of TGF-beta, certain macrophage populations actively enforce tumor cell quiescence. These signals modulate cyclin-dependent kinase inhibitors and maintain DTCs in a non-proliferative state.
- **Reactivation-promoting macrophages**: M2-like TAMs can create pro-survival niches that may facilitate eventual DTC reawakening through inflammatory signaling, ECM remodeling, and angiogenic factors.

A therapy that indiscriminately eliminates all macrophages (e.g., CSF1R inhibitors) could:
1. Remove dormancy-maintaining signals (TGF-beta, WNT inhibitors), potentially triggering DTC reactivation
2. Simultaneously remove immunosuppressive barriers, enhancing immune surveillance
3. The net effect is unpredictable and likely context-dependent (organ-specific, cancer-type specific)

### 6.2 Temporal Dynamics

The timing of macrophage-targeted therapy relative to dormancy phases is critical:
- **During dormancy**: Intervening to maintain or reinforce dormancy requires preserving or enhancing dormancy-maintaining macrophage signals while eliminating those that create pre-metastatic niches favorable for reactivation.
- **At the point of reactivation**: Aggressive TAM depletion or repolarization could potentially prevent the angiogenic switch and inflammatory cascade needed for metastatic outgrowth.

### 6.3 Compensatory Mechanisms

CSF1R inhibition can lead to compensatory recruitment of other immunosuppressive myeloid cells (MDSCs, neutrophils), which may fill the niche vacated by macrophages and potentially create conditions favorable for DTC reactivation.

### 6.4 Off-Target and Systemic Effects

- CSF1R inhibitors cause transaminase elevations (observed with both pexidartinib and eganelisib), reflecting Kupffer cell depletion in the liver. Since the liver is a common site of DTC dissemination, altering hepatic macrophage populations could have unpredictable effects on hepatic dormancy niches.
- PI3K-gamma inhibition has more targeted effects on macrophage polarization rather than depletion, potentially offering a safer profile in the dormancy context.

### 6.5 Lack of Clinical Models

No established clinical model or trial design specifically tests the effect of macrophage-targeted therapy on dormancy endpoints. Dormancy operates on timescales of years to decades, making clinical testing extremely challenging. The adjuvant setting (patients with no evidence of disease after primary tumor resection, but at high risk for recurrence) would be the appropriate context, but requires large, long-duration trials with disease-free survival endpoints.

### 6.6 Biomarker Limitations

Current biomarkers cannot reliably distinguish dormancy-maintaining from reactivation-promoting macrophage subsets in patients. Without this resolution, therapeutic targeting risks collateral damage to protective macrophage populations.

## Summary and Recommendations

1. **Repolarization over depletion**: The available evidence suggests that repolarization strategies (PI3K-gamma inhibitors, STING agonists) are likely safer than wholesale macrophage depletion (CSF1R inhibitors) in the dormancy context, as they preserve macrophage presence while shifting functional phenotype.

2. **Combination approaches are essential**: The MARIO-3 data support triple combinations (macrophage reprogramming + ICI + chemotherapy) as the most promising approach, particularly in the adjuvant setting for high-risk patients.

3. **The CXCL9/SPP1 axis and spatial transcriptomics** represent the most promising biomarker approaches for monitoring macrophage polarization in clinical trials, and should be incorporated into any dormancy-focused trial design.

4. **The dormancy paradox must be addressed experimentally**: Preclinical models that specifically test whether macrophage depletion or repolarization affects DTC dormancy maintenance vs. reactivation are urgently needed before advancing to clinical trials.

5. **STING agonist + CSF1R inhibitor combinations** (as demonstrated by Yang et al., 2025) represent a mechanistically sophisticated approach that merits specific investigation in dormancy models, as the sequential strategy could potentially deplete reactivation-promoting TAMs while repolarizing dormancy-maintaining TAMs.
