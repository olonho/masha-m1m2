## 1. Repolarization Mechanisms

### 1.1 M2-to-M1 Repolarization

- **IFN-gamma and LPS**: Classical M1 inducers via JAK/STAT1 and TLR4/MyD88/NF-kappaB
- **CD40 agonists**: Reprogram immunosuppressive TAMs toward inflammatory M1-like phenotype
- **STING agonists**: Trigger type I interferon production and innate immune activation (Li et al., 2024, Advanced Materials)
- **PI3K-gamma inhibition**: Eganelisib (IPI-549) repolarizes TAMs; O'Connell et al. (2024, PMID 39214650) confirmed in clinical trial biopsies

### 1.2 M1-to-M2 Repolarization

- **IL-4/IL-13**: STAT6-driven M2 polarization, upregulating ARG1, CD206, CD163
- **IL-10 and TGF-beta**: STAT3 and SMAD signaling enforcing immunosuppressive M2 polarization
- **Glucocorticoids**: Clinically relevant doses may inadvertently reinforce M2-dominated dormant niches
- **Hypoxia**: HIF-1alpha-driven M2 polarization via VEGF, ARG1, CD206
- **Adenosine**: A2A receptor/cAMP/PKA/CREB signaling inducing M2 polarization

### 1.3 Metabolic Switches

- **Glycolysis vs. OXPHOS**: M1 relies on aerobic glycolysis; M2 depends on OXPHOS and FAO
- **Itaconate**: IRG1/ACOD1 product in M1 macrophages; inhibits SDH, prevents succinate accumulation
- **Succinate**: Accumulates in M1 macrophages; stabilizes HIF-1alpha, drives IL-1beta
- **Arginine metabolism**: M1 expresses iNOS (NO production); M2 expresses ARG1 (ornithine/urea)

### 1.4 Epigenetic Regulation

- **Histone modifications**: HDAC2-SP1 axis (Zheng et al., 2023, Cancer Discovery); HDAC inhibitors drive durable M2-to-M1 switch
- **DNA methylation**: DNMT3a-mediated methylation of M1 gene promoters creates epigenetic "memory"
- **Chromatin remodeling**: SWI/SNF complex and PU.1 regulate macrophage polarization

## 2. Plasticity Spectrum: Is the M1/M2 Binary Obsolete?

### 2.1 Evidence for a Continuum

Mitsui and Satoh (2025, PMID 41035106) explicitly state M1/M2 model is inadequate. TAMs exist along a multidimensional phenotypic continuum.

### 2.2 Emerging scRNA-seq-Defined TAM Subsets

- **TREM2+ TAMs**: Conserved lipid-associated subset, immunosuppressive, pro-tumorigenic (Coulton et al., 2024, PMID 38969631)
- **SPP1+ TAMs**: ECM remodeling and pro-metastatic functions
- **FOLR2+ TAMs**: Tissue-resident lineage, ontogenically distinct from monocyte-derived (Rakina et al., 2024, PMID 38571605)
- **Resident vs. monocyte-derived TAMs**: Dalla et al. (2024, Cell) demonstrated alveolar macrophages maintain dormancy, recruited monocyte-derived promote outgrowth

## 3. Repolarization and Dormancy

### 3.1 M2-to-M1 Repolarization: Effects on Dormant DTCs

Walker et al. (2019, PMID 30683851) established:
- M2 macrophages maintain breast cancer dormancy via gap junctional intercellular communication (GJIC)
- M1 macrophages reverse dormancy through NF-kappaB-activating exosomes
- TLR4 activation switches M2 to M1, producing exosomes that break dormancy

### 3.2 M1-to-M2 Shifts

M2-like macrophages secrete TGF-beta2, BMP4/7, WNT5A enforcing DTC quiescence via NR2F1, DEC2, p27.

Pereira et al. (2025, Cell Reports) identified inflammatory cytokines mediating induction of and awakening from metastatic dormancy.

### 3.3 Intermediate Polarization States

In vivo macrophages rarely exist in purely M1 or M2 states. The key question is the net signaling output — more TGF-beta (dormancy-maintaining) or more TNF-alpha (dormancy-disrupting)?

### 3.4 Macrophage Extracellular Vesicles and Exosomes

- M1 exosomes carry NF-kappaB-activating molecules that break dormancy
- M2 exosomes carry dormancy-maintaining miRNAs (miR-21, miR-29a) and signaling molecules via GJIC

## 5. Therapeutic Repolarization Strategies

### 5.1 CD47-SIRPalpha Blockade
Sun et al. (2025, Mol Cancer, PMID 41163221): targeting CXCR2 blocks CD47-SIRPalpha and reverses M2 polarization.

### 5.2 CD40 Agonists
McVey and Beatty (2025): CD40 agonists convert TAMs to tumoricidal M1-like states.

### 5.3 PI3K-gamma Inhibitors
Eganelisib (IPI-549): most clinically advanced selective repolarization agent.

### 5.4 CSF1R Inhibitors
Deplete TAMs rather than repolarize; carry dormancy-disruption risk.

### 5.5 Bisphosphonates
Zoledronic acid depletes osteoclasts; may alter bone marrow niche dormancy signaling.

## 6. Feedback Loops and Crosstalk

### 6.1 How DTCs Influence Macrophage Polarization

- CSF1 secretion recruits monocytes and promotes M2 differentiation
- CCL2/MCP-1 recruits CCR2+ monocytes
- IL-6/JAK2/STAT3 axis suppresses miR-34a, promotes SNAIL-driven EMT
- CD47 upregulation on dormant DTCs evades phagocytosis

### 6.2 Bidirectional Signaling Loops

- **Loop 1**: TGF-beta amplification — M2 TAMs secrete TGF-beta → dormancy → more TGF-beta
- **Loop 2**: EGF/CSF1 paracrine loop — macrophage EGF → tumor CSF1 → more M2 macrophages
- **Loop 3**: NF-kappaB/IL-1beta inflammatory amplification — M1 TNF-alpha/IL-1beta → DTC NF-kappaB → dormancy exit
- **Loop 4**: Exosome-mediated signaling — M2 exosomes maintain dormancy; M1 exosomes break it

### 6.3 Crosstalk with Other Immune and Stromal Cells

- Macrophage-T cell: M1 TAMs prime CD8+ T cells; M2 TAMs recruit Tregs via CCL22/IL-10
- Macrophage-NK cell: M2 TAMs suppress NK function via TGF-beta and PGE2
- Macrophage-fibroblast: M2 TAMs activate fibroblasts, creating physical niche for dormant DTCs
