## SECTION 1: NICHE-SPECIFIC MACROPHAGE BEHAVIOR

### Bone Marrow Niche

The bone marrow harbors a specialized perivascular niche where DTCs can remain dormant for years. Bone marrow macrophages (osteomacs) and osteoclasts (macrophage-lineage cells) interact with osteoblasts and MSCs to regulate DTC fate. Jin et al. (2025, PMID: 40886737) reviewed osteoblast contributions to bone metastasis, describing how osteoblast-derived CXCL12 engages CXCR4 on DTCs to promote quiescence, while TGF-beta2 and BMP7 from the niche actively enforce dormancy. Cassetta & Pollard (2023, PMID: 36792751) noted that metastasis-associated macrophages "maintain tumour cell dormancy in some contexts."

### Lung Niche

He et al. (2025, PMID: 40614736) demonstrated in *Cancer Cell* that chemotherapy (platinum-based agents) induces NETs in the lung, which awaken dormant cancer cells. TAMs synergize with NETs: macrophage-derived inflammatory mediators prime the niche, while NET-associated proteases (NE, MMP9) degrade the dormancy-sustaining ECM.

### Liver Niche

Correia et al. (2021, *Nature* 594:566-571) demonstrated that hepatic stellate cells (HSCs) suppress NK cell-mediated killing, sustaining breast cancer dormancy. Kupffer cells interact with HSCs and SECs to modulate immune surveillance.

### Brain Niche

Dai et al. (2022, PMID: 35121993) demonstrated in *Nature Cancer* that astrocyte-derived laminin-211 drives breast tumor cell dormancy in the brain via the dystroglycan receptor and downstream YAP signaling.

## SECTION 2: EXTRACELLULAR MATRIX AND STRUCTURAL CUES

### Type III Collagen

Di Martino et al. (2022, PMID: 35121989) demonstrated in *Nature Cancer* that tumor-derived type III collagen creates a dormancy-enforcing ECM niche. Macrophage-derived MMPs (MMP-9, MMP-13) can degrade this collagen scaffold.

### Laminin-211 and the Perivascular Niche

Astrocyte-derived laminin-211 provides a structural cue for dormancy in the brain. Macrophage-derived MMPs (MMP-2, MMP-9) can cleave laminin, generating fragments that lose dormancy-sustaining capacity.

### Fibronectin and Periostin

Macrophages and fibroblasts deposit fibronectin and periostin. Periostin engages integrins on DTCs, activating FAK and PI3K/AKT signaling that can overcome dormancy.

### TGF-beta Activation from ECM

Macrophages activate latent TGF-beta stored in the ECM through integrin alphaVbeta8 and MMP-mediated cleavage.

## SECTION 3: CROSSTALK WITH OTHER NICHE CELLS

### Macrophage-Fibroblast Crosstalk

Watson et al. (2024, PMID: 39255775) demonstrated in *Cancer Cell* that anti-CSF-1R therapy in glioblastoma triggers a fibrotic response that potentiates recurrence.

### Macrophage-MSC/Osteoblast Interactions

CXCL12/CXCR4 signaling between MSCs/osteoblasts and DTCs maintains quiescence. GAS6/MERTK signaling provides additional survival signals.

### Macrophage-Endothelial Cell Interactions and Angiogenic Dormancy

M2-like TAMs produce VEGF, PlGF, and angiopoietin-2 that promote the angiogenic switch. M1-like macrophages produce TSP-1, sustaining avascular dormant state.

## SECTION 5: HYPOXIA AND METABOLIC REGULATION

- Hypoxia drives M2 polarization via HIF-1alpha/HIF-2alpha
- Metabolic competition: dormant DTCs are metabolically quiescent
- Adenosine-CD39-CD73 feed-forward loop reinforces M2 polarization
- Lactate and succinate as signaling molecules
