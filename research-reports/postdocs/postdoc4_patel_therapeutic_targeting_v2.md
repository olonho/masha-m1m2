## 1. CSF1R Inhibitors in Solid Tumors: Clinical and Preclinical Evidence Relevant to Dormancy and Metastatic Recurrence

### 1.1 Background

Colony-stimulating factor 1 receptor (CSF1R) signaling is the primary axis governing macrophage survival, proliferation, and recruitment. In the tumor microenvironment (TME), CSF1/CSF1R signaling drives the accumulation of immunosuppressive, M2-polarized tumor-associated macrophages (TAMs) that promote immune evasion, angiogenesis, and metastatic outgrowth. Three CSF1R-targeting agents have reached advanced clinical development: **pexidartinib** (small-molecule tyrosine kinase inhibitor, FDA-approved for tenosynovial giant cell tumor), **emactuzumab** (anti-CSF1R monoclonal antibody), and **cabiralizumab** (anti-CSF1R monoclonal antibody, also known as FPA-144).

### 1.2 Clinical Evidence

**Pexidartinib.** The ENLIVEN Phase III trial established efficacy in TGCT, but its application in solid tumors remains investigational. A critical translational finding was reported by **Voissiere et al. (2024, Science Translational Medicine)**, who demonstrated that pexidartinib not only depletes TAMs but also impairs FLT3-dependent dendritic cell differentiation, potentially antagonizing the efficacy of concurrent anti-PD-L1 therapy (durvalumab). This finding has major implications for combination strategies in the dormancy context — broad myeloid depletion may inadvertently cripple the antigen-presenting arm needed to surveil dormant DTCs.

**Cabiralizumab.** **Foster et al. (2021, Clinical Cancer Research)** conducted a Phase I study combining cabiralizumab with stereotactic body radiotherapy (SBRT) and nivolumab in advanced solid tumors. While the combination was tolerable, objective responses were modest. Importantly, pharmacodynamic analyses confirmed TAM depletion in on-treatment biopsies but did not specifically address dormant DTC reservoirs or minimal residual disease (MRD) states.

**Emactuzumab.** Early-phase studies demonstrated robust TAM depletion (>80% reduction in CSF1R-positive cells) in multiple tumor types, but as monotherapy, objective responses were rare.

**CSF1R inhibition in HNSCC.** **Chen et al. (2025, Journal of Translational Medicine)** provided a comprehensive analysis of CSF1R inhibition for TAM modulation in head and neck squamous cell carcinoma (32 citations), demonstrating that TAM reprogramming improved response to immune checkpoint inhibitors in preclinical models, though dormancy-specific endpoints were not assessed.

### 1.3 Relevance to Dormancy

The dormancy-relevant insight from CSF1R inhibitor studies is nuanced. Complete TAM depletion may **remove both pro-dormancy and pro-outgrowth macrophage subsets**. The landmark study by **Borriello et al. (2022, Nature Communications)** demonstrated that a specific subset of macrophages within the primary tumor actively induces both pro-dissemination AND pro-dormancy phenotypes in DTCs. This implies that indiscriminate TAM depletion could paradoxically eliminate macrophage-derived dormancy-enforcing signals (e.g., TGF-beta2, BMP7, WNT5A) while also removing proliferative cues. The net effect on dormant DTC fate is unpredictable and likely context-dependent.

## 2. Macrophage Repolarization Agents: Evidence Affecting Dormancy

### 2.1 CD40 Agonists

CD40 agonists activate antigen-presenting cells and reprogram immunosuppressive TAMs toward an inflammatory, anti-tumor (M1-like) phenotype. **McVey and Beatty (2025, Clinical Cancer Research)** provided a comprehensive review of CD40 agonists in cancer immunotherapy (29 citations), cataloguing both the promise and the translational challenges. Key findings relevant to dormancy:

- CD40 activation converts TAMs from an M2-like, immunosuppressive state to an M1-like, tumoricidal state capable of direct tumor cell killing and T-cell priming.
- In preclinical models of pancreatic ductal adenocarcinoma (PDAC), CD40 agonist therapy induced macrophage-mediated tumor regressions even in the absence of T cells.
- **Dormancy implication:** If dormant DTCs are maintained in quiescence by M2-like TAM-derived signals (TGF-beta, IL-10), repolarization to M1-like phenotypes via CD40 activation could disrupt the dormancy-enforcing niche. Whether this leads to beneficial immune-mediated clearance or dangerous awakening is a critical unresolved question.

### 2.2 TLR Agonists

Toll-like receptor agonists (TLR3: poly(I:C); TLR7/8: imiquimod, resiquimod; TLR9: CpG-ODN) activate innate immune sensing and promote M1 polarization. Intratumoral TLR9 agonists (e.g., tilsotolimod) have been tested in combination with anti-CTLA-4 in melanoma.

### 2.3 PI3K-gamma Inhibitors (Eganelisib / IPI-549)

PI3K-gamma is predominantly expressed in myeloid cells and serves as a critical molecular switch between immunosuppressive and immunostimulatory macrophage states.

- **O'Connell et al. (2024)** reported on eganelisib combined with immune checkpoint inhibition and chemotherapy in frontline metastatic triple-negative breast cancer (TNBC) (70 citations). Pharmacodynamic analyses confirmed TAM repolarization in on-treatment biopsies.
- **Mognol et al. (2025, Trends in Cancer)** reviewed the broader landscape of PI3K-gamma targeting in cancer (14 citations).
- **Xu et al. (2024, Cancer Immunology)** conducted a systematic review and meta-analysis of PI3K-gamma in myeloid-mediated immunosuppression (29 citations).

### 2.4 HDAC Inhibitors (Class I)

**Zheng et al. (2023, Cancer Discovery)** made a seminal contribution, demonstrating that the HDAC2-SP1 axis orchestrates protumor macrophage polarization. Class I HDAC inhibitors (particularly entinostat, mocetinostat) induced a durable M2-to-M1 switch in TAMs (55 citations).

### 2.5 STING Agonists

- **Li et al. (2024, Advanced Materials)** reported on photoactivated STING agonists designed to repolarize TAMs in situ (32 citations).
- **Xu et al. (2025, Signal Transduction and Targeted Therapy)** provided a comprehensive review of the dual roles and therapeutic targeting of TAMs (212 citations).

## 3. Combination Strategies Aimed at Preventing Metastatic Relapse

### 3.1 Macrophage-Targeting + Immune Checkpoint Inhibition

The dominant combination paradigm in clinical development is TAM modulation combined with anti-PD-1/PD-L1 or anti-CTLA-4 antibodies.

### 3.2 Macrophage-Targeting + Chemotherapy

The **Eganelisib + atezolizumab + nab-paclitaxel** in TNBC (O'Connell et al., 2024) represents the most advanced test of this triple combination.

### 3.3 Macrophage-Targeting + Radiotherapy

- **Foster et al. (2021)** established the safety of SBRT + nivolumab + cabiralizumab.

### 3.4 Proposed Dormancy-Specific Combination Framework

- **Maintain dormancy strategy:** TGF-beta pathway maintenance + selective M2-to-M1 repolarization (PI3K-gamma inhibition)
- **Clear-and-eliminate strategy:** CD40 agonist + anti-PD-1 + HDAC inhibitor to awaken and clear DTCs

## 5. Biomarkers for Monitoring Macrophage Polarization States

### 5.1 Imaging Biomarkers
- TSPO-PET for activated macrophages
- Ferumoxytol-enhanced MRI for TAM distribution
- CEACAM1 and CD206-targeted probes

### 5.2 Circulating Biomarkers
- Soluble CD163 (sCD163) and soluble CD206
- Circulating cytokine panels (IL-10/TNF-alpha, TGF-beta/IL-12 ratios)
- Circulating tumor DNA (ctDNA) paired with macrophage markers

### 5.3 Single-Cell RNA Sequencing Signatures
- Klughammer et al. (2024, Nature Medicine): TAM subsets (SPP1+, C1Q+, CXCL10+, MARCO+)
- Spatial transcriptomics (Visium, MERFISH, CODEX)
- CEBPB-high dormant tumor cells (Bai et al., 2026, Theranostics)

## 6. Challenges and Risks

### 6.1 The Paradox of TAM Depletion
- Loss of dormancy enforcement (TGF-beta2, BMP7, WNT5A)
- Loss of immune surveillance (antigen presentation)
- Niche destabilization

### 6.2 The Risk of Inflammatory Awakening via Repolarization
- Incomplete clearance of awakened DTCs
- Inflammatory growth factor release (EGF, HGF, VEGF)
- NF-kappaB activation in DTCs driving dormancy exit

### 6.3 The Dose-Timing Problem
No validated clinical method to determine dormancy status of individual DTCs.

### 6.4 The Stromal Compensation Problem
Macrophage depletion triggers compensatory signaling from fibroblasts, MSCs, osteoblasts.

### 6.5 Clinical Translation Gap
No macrophage-targeting therapy tested with dormancy-specific endpoints.

### 6.6 Summary Risk Assessment

| Strategy | Risk of Awakening DTCs | Risk of Inadequate Clearance | Net Assessment |
|----------|----------------------|------------------------------|----------------|
| CSF1R inhibitor | HIGH | MODERATE | Risky as monotherapy |
| CD40 agonist | HIGH | LOW-MODERATE | Acceptable if combined with effective clearance |
| PI3K-gamma inhibitor | MODERATE | LOW | Most favorable risk-benefit profile |
| HDAC inhibitor | MODERATE | MODERATE | Promising; needs dormancy-specific testing |
| STING agonist | HIGH | LOW | High-risk, high-reward |

## Conclusions and Research Priorities

1. **Selective repolarization over depletion.**
2. **Dormancy-specific preclinical models are essential.**
3. **Spatial multi-omics of the dormant niche.**
4. **Adjuvant clinical trials with dormancy endpoints.**
5. **Coupled biomarker development.**
