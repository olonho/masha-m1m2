## PART B: CLAIM DOWNGRADES

### B1. "Macrophage polarization is a central rheostat"

- **Current language:** "central rheostat governing DTC dormancy entry, maintenance, and escape"
- **Corrected language:** "a significant modulator of DTC dormancy, with the strongest evidence in the lung niche (Dalla et al. 2024); evidence in other organ niches is incomplete, indirect, or absent"
- **Applies to:** Moretti report, Klein report, all executive summaries
- **Rationale:** Strong causal evidence exists only for lung (Dalla et al. 2024). Liver niche centrality is about the HSC-NK axis (Correia et al. 2021), not macrophages. Brain niche has no primary data. Bone marrow relies on reviews.

### B2. "TGF-beta is the single most important dormancy cytokine"

- **Current language:** "the single most important dormancy cytokine"
- **Corrected language:** "among the most consistently implicated dormancy-associated cytokines"
- **Applies to:** Moretti report (Section III.2), all documents making this claim
- **Rationale:** TGF-beta isoforms have dual roles (TGF-beta2/RIII maintains dormancy; TGF-beta1/NRP2 promotes escape). IFN-gamma has equally strong mechanistic evidence (Pereira et al. 2025). WNT5A, BMP4/7, and CXCL10 also have dormancy-enforcing evidence. Isoform-specific, receptor-specific, and organ-specific effects make single-cytokine primacy claims unwarranted.

### B3. Premature Clinical Development Roadmap

- **Current state:** Klein report Section VII proposes a Phase 1-4 clinical development roadmap including a Phase 2 adjuvant trial of eganelisib.
- **Required action:** REMOVE the entire clinical development roadmap. Replace with a prioritized list of preclinical experiments needed before any clinical trial could be designed.
- **Rationale:** Zero dormancy-specific preclinical data for any macrophage-targeting agent. Eganelisib developer (Infinity Pharmaceuticals) has unclear operational status. No IRB would approve a Phase 2 adjuvant trial based on current evidence.
- **Applies to:** Klein report Section VII

### B4. M1/M2 Binary Language

- **Current state:** Reports acknowledge M1/M2 binary is obsolete but continue using it as organizing framework.
- **Required action:** Qualify all M1/M2 language with explicit acknowledgment of the spectrum model. Add a footnote or parenthetical statement: "M1 and M2 designations are used here as shorthand for ends of a polarization continuum, not as discrete states. The spectrum model (TREM2+, SPP1+, FOLR2+ TAMs; resident vs. monocyte-derived) better captures functional diversity."
- **Applies to:** All documents

### B5. Chemotherapy Paradox Scope

- **Current language:** Implies broad chemotherapy effect on dormancy escape
- **Corrected language:** Confine specifically to taxanes (Ganesan et al. 2023, PLoS Biol). IL-6/G-CSF/MEK/ERK activation mechanism demonstrated for taxane-induced stromal injury only. Other chemotherapy classes (anthracyclines, platinum agents, anti-metabolites) have not been tested.
- **Additional requirement:** Acknowledge that clinical trial data (ECOG 1199 and other large adjuvant taxane trials) do not show increased distant recurrence with taxane-containing regimens.
- **Applies to:** Moretti report (Section III.3), Bergstrom report, Klein report

### B6. CD68/CD163 Ratio as "Validated" Biomarker

- **Current language:** Various reports present HR 10.32 as robust evidence
- **Corrected language:** "Preliminary finding; HR 10.32 (95% CI 1.34-80.0) based on 13 recurrence events in 67 patients (Darvishian et al. 2022). Requires independent validation in larger cohorts before any clinical application."
- **Remove:** The word "validated" from all biomarker discussions
- **Applies to:** Klein report, Chen report, Bergstrom report

### B7. "Selective macrophage repolarization is strongly preferred"

- **Current language:** "strongly preferred over broad depletion"
- **Corrected language:** "theoretically preferable but currently unachievable due to the inability to identify dormancy-enforcing vs. dormancy-breaking macrophage subsets"
- **Applies to:** Klein report executive summary, Section II

### B8. Eganelisib as "Lead Therapeutic Candidate"

- **Current language:** "lead therapeutic candidate"
- **Corrected language:** "candidate warranting dormancy-specific preclinical testing"
- **Additional requirement:** Explicitly state that the risk-benefit framework is theoretical (based on mechanism-of-action reasoning), not empirical. No dormancy-specific data exist for any agent.
- **Applies to:** Klein report, Bergstrom report
