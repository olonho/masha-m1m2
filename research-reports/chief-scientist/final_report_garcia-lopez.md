## 1. EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

This report constitutes a comprehensive critical review of seven team submissions -- five postdoctoral briefings and two senior scientist syntheses -- addressing the role of macrophage polarization in regulating dormant disseminated tumor cells (DTCs). The review was conducted with full PubMed-level verification of all cited references, rigorous cross-comparison of claims across reports, and uncompromising scrutiny of methodological and logical rigor.

**Principal findings of this review:**

- The team has assembled a substantial and largely coherent body of evidence supporting the centrality of macrophage polarization states in DTC dormancy maintenance versus escape.
- **Six citations are UNVERIFIABLE** -- they return zero results in PubMed under any reasonable search strategy. One (He et al. 2026) bears a future publication date and is almost certainly fabricated or hallucinated.
- **One confirmed reference (Watson et al. 2024) is applied outside its validated context** -- it studied glioblastoma, not breast cancer DTC dormancy, yet was cited as if its findings were directly transferable.
- The M1/M2 binary framework, while acknowledged as inadequate by multiple team members, persists as the organizing language across reports, creating a contradiction between stated awareness and actual practice.
- Organ-specific niche biology is the strongest and most novel component of the team's output, yet it remains insufficiently integrated into the therapeutic synthesis.
- The eganelisib (PI3K-gamma inhibitor) therapeutic pathway is the most clinically advanced but faces unresolved contradictions regarding whether PI3K-gamma inhibition promotes dormancy maintenance or inadvertently facilitates niche remodeling.
- Bidirectional signaling loops between tumor cells and macrophages are inadequately formalized, despite being mentioned by multiple authors.
- The surgical vulnerability window (1 week to 6 months post-resection) is identified but its mechanistic basis in macrophage biology remains superficially treated.

**Overall Assessment:** The research direction is sound and has translational promise. However, the presence of unverifiable citations, context-stretched references, and insufficient cross-ordination between the signaling biology and therapeutic teams constitutes a serious lapse that must be corrected before any external submission or grant application.

## 2. CRITICAL ASSESSMENT OF TEAM REPORTS

### 2.1 Dr. Maria Vasquez -- Signaling Pathways

**Strengths:**
- Correct identification of the ERK/p38 ratio as the central dormancy signaling hub is well-supported by the literature.
- The NR2F1 master regulator concept is appropriately positioned and supported by Borriello et al. 2022 (confirmed: Nat Commun 13(1):589, PMID 35110548).
- The CXCL12/CXCR4 axis in bone marrow dormancy maintenance is well-established and correctly presented.
- Recognition that M1-type macrophage secretome (TNF-alpha, IL-12, IFN-gamma) promotes dormancy via p38 activation is mechanistically coherent.

**Critical Deficiencies:**

1. **Insufficient nuance on the ERK/p38 ratio.** The briefing treats this ratio as a binary switch rather than a dynamic equilibrium with stochastic fluctuation. Recent single-cell data (Recalde-Percaz et al. 2025, confirmed: Neoplasia 68, PMID 40848614) demonstrate heterogeneous ERK/p38 states within individual DTC populations. This heterogeneity is critical because it means that a subpopulation of DTCs may be poised for exit even when the "average" signaling profile suggests dormancy. Dr. Vasquez must address this.

2. **The TGF-beta1-NRP2 axis is presented without adequate consideration of context-dependency.** TGF-beta is perhaps the most pleiotropic cytokine in cancer biology -- it can maintain dormancy in some contexts and promote EMT and escape in others. The briefing does not grapple with this duality. The Dalla et al. 2024 paper (confirmed: Cell 187(23):6631-6648.e20, PMID 39378878) specifically shows that TGF-betaRIII on alveolar macrophages maintains breast cancer cell dormancy in the lung. This is a crucial organ-specific modifier of TGF-beta signaling that was not integrated.

3. **The DDR1/STAT1/type III collagen pathway** is mentioned but insufficiently developed. This is one of the most compelling mechanistic links between extracellular matrix architecture and dormancy maintenance. The briefing should have provided more detail on how macrophage-derived signals interact with the collagen III scaffold.

4. **Missing: quantitative data.** No IC50 values, no fold-change magnitudes, no dose-response relationships. A signaling pathway briefing without quantitative parameters is a narrative, not a scientific document.

**Grade: B-** -- Mechanistically sound framework but lacks the quantitative rigor and context-dependent nuance expected at this level.

### 2.2 Dr. Adaeze Okonkwo -- Organ-Specific Niches

**Strengths:**
- This is the most intellectually ambitious and structurally novel of the postdoctoral briefings. The organ-by-organ comparison framework is exactly what the field needs.
- Bone marrow niche (CXCL12/CXCR4, osteoblast-macrophage crosstalk) is well-presented.
- Lung niche coverage is significantly enhanced by the incorporation of Dalla et al. 2024 (confirmed), which specifically identifies TGF-betaRIII on alveolar macrophages as a dormancy-maintaining receptor.
- Liver niche (Kupffer cell / HSC / NK cell axis) is correctly identified as the least well-characterized.
- Brain niche (microglia / laminin-211 / YAP signaling) is a novel inclusion that pushes beyond the standard organ repertoire.

**Critical Deficiencies:**

1. **The He et al. 2026 citation is unverifiable and almost certainly fabricated.** This reference, cited as "He et al. 2026, J Hematol Oncol," returns zero results in PubMed. The year 2026 is the current year. Unless this is a preprint with a specific DOI that was provided separately, it cannot be cited in any formal document. This is a **serious breach of citation integrity** and must be retracted from the briefing immediately.

2. **Insufficient attention to niche plasticity.** The briefing treats each organ niche as relatively static, but niches themselves are remodeled by tumor-derived signals, systemic inflammation, and therapeutic interventions. The concept of "niche flipping" -- where a dormancy-maintaining niche is converted to a permissive niche by macrophage repolarization -- is implied but never formally developed.

3. **Cross-organ comparison is descriptive, not analytical.** The briefing lists what happens in each organ but does not systematically compare signaling pathways, macrophage phenotypes, or dormancy exit mechanisms across organs. A comparison matrix is needed.

4. **The Tang et al. 2020 citation is unverifiable.** Zero PubMed results under any reasonable search strategy.

5. **Missing: peritoneal and adrenal niches.** These are recognized sites of DTC dormancy in ovarian and breast cancer respectively. Their omission leaves the organ-specific analysis incomplete.

**Grade: B** -- Strong conceptual framework undermined by unverifiable citations and insufficient analytical cross-comparison.

### 2.3 Dr. Wei Chen -- Therapeutic Strategies

**Strengths:**
- The CSF-1R inhibition paradox is correctly identified and well-articulated. This is a genuinely important observation: depleting TAMs may eliminate the very macrophage populations that maintain dormancy, triggering exit. The fibrosis angle is particularly well-taken.
- PI3K-gamma inhibition via eganelisib is appropriately positioned as the lead therapeutic candidate, with clinical trial data (MARIO-1: Hong et al. 2023, confirmed, Clin Cancer Res, PMID 37000164; MARIO-3: O'Connell et al. 2024, confirmed, J Immunother Cancer, PMID 39214650) providing translational credibility.
- The HDAC2-SP1 epigenetic axis concept is innovative and links macrophage polarization reprogramming to dormancy maintenance through a mechanistic chain.

**Critical Deficiencies:**

1. **The eganelisib contradiction is insufficiently addressed.** PI3K-gamma inhibition reprograms immunosuppressive (M2-like) macrophages toward an immunostimulatory (M1-like) phenotype. If M1-type signals promote dormancy (as Dr. Vasquez argues), then eganelisib should promote dormancy maintenance. But M2-to-M1 repolarization also increases inflammatory cytokines and tissue remodeling factors that could disrupt the dormancy niche. The briefing acknowledges the CSF-1R paradox but does not apply the same critical logic to PI3K-gamma inhibition. This inconsistency must be resolved.

2. **The Sun et al. 2023 citation is unverifiable.** Zero PubMed results.

3. **Walker et al. 2019 is unverifiable.** Zero PubMed results.

4. **Clinical trial data is presented without critical appraisal.** MARIO-1 (Phase I) demonstrated safety and some efficacy signals. MARIO-3 (Phase Ib/II) combined eganelisib with atezolizumab and nab-paclitaxel. Neither trial was designed to test dormancy maintenance. Dr. Chen implies clinical relevance for dormancy that the trial data simply do not support. This is over-interpretation.

5. **The pro-resolving macrophage / FLN (fibrotic-like niche) concept** is intriguing but presented with insufficient mechanistic detail. How precisely do pro-resolving macrophages target the fibrotic niche? Through what receptors and effector pathways? The briefing is silent on these questions.

6. **Darvishian et al. 2022 is unverifiable.** Zero PubMed results.

**Grade: C+** -- Important therapeutic concepts but undermined by three unverifiable citations, over-interpretation of clinical data, and an unresolved mechanistic contradiction regarding PI3K-gamma inhibition.

### 2.4 Dr. Raj Patel -- Emerging Mechanisms

**Strengths:**
- The NET-TAM (neutrophil extracellular trap / tumor-associated macrophage) synergy concept for dormancy exit is genuinely novel and well-sourced. NETs trap DTCs in a pro-inflammatory microenvironment that synergizes with M2-polarized TAMs to promote reactivation. This is a legitimate emerging mechanism.
- Metabolic reprogramming (glycolysis/OXPHOS balance, itaconate, succinate) is correctly identified as an under-explored dimension of macrophage-DTC crosstalk.
- The bidirectional signaling concept -- tumor cells educating macrophages and vice versa -- is correctly highlighted as a critical gap in the field.
- TMEM (Tumor Microenvironment of Metastasis) doorways priming DTCs for dissemination, supported by Borriello et al. 2022 (confirmed), is properly incorporated.

**Critical Deficiencies:**

1. **The Pereira et al. 2025 Cell Reports citation is unverifiable.** PubMed returns 22 papers by authors named Pereira in Cell Reports for 2025, but none address macrophage polarization and DTC dormancy. This is either a fabricated reference or a severe misattribution.

2. **The DNMT3a "locking" claim is unverifiable in context.** DNMT3a is indeed involved in macrophage polarization (Li et al. 2020 FASEB J, PMID 32052888; Cui et al. 2022 Eur J Pharmacol, PMID 35597264), but these studies concern atherosclerosis and hepatic fibrosis, not DTC dormancy. The specific claim that DNMT3a epigenetically "locks" macrophage polarization in the dormancy niche has no identifiable source. Dr. Patel must either provide the specific citation or retract this claim.

3. **Excessive speculation without adequate grounding.** The briefing is the most speculative of the five postdoctoral submissions. While emerging mechanisms naturally involve more conjecture, the briefing crosses the line between informed hypothesis and unsupported assertion, particularly in the metabolic reprogramming section where causal claims are made from correlative data.

4. **NET-TAM synergy is presented as a one-directional mechanism.** The briefing does not consider whether dormant DTCs actively suppress NET formation or neutrophil recruitment, which would constitute a tumor-intrinsic dormancy maintenance mechanism.

**Grade: B-** -- Genuinely novel concepts, particularly NET-TAM synergy, but undermined by unverifiable citations and insufficient distinction between hypothesis and evidence.

### 2.5 Dr. Erik Bergstrom -- Translational Integration

**Strengths:**
- The attempt to bridge bench findings to clinical application is laudable and necessary.
- The surgical vulnerability window (1 week to 6 months post-resection) is an important translational concept that connects perioperative immune dynamics to dormancy exit.
- Integration of clinical trial data (MARIO-1, MARIO-3) with preclinical dormancy models is a useful exercise.
- Recognition that tissue injury and wound healing responses during surgery create a temporary window of macrophage dysregulation is clinically relevant.

**Critical Deficiencies:**

1. **The Ganesan et al. 2023 PLOS Biology citation is CONFIRMED (PMID 37699010) but MISAPPLIED.** The paper exists and is relevant -- "Taxane chemotherapy induces stromal injury that leads to breast cancer dormancy escape" (PLoS Biol. 2023 Sep 12;21(9):e3002275). However, the paper's focus is on taxane-induced stromal injury, not on macrophage polarization per se. Dr. Bergstrom cites it as if it directly supports claims about macrophage-mediated dormancy escape, when the paper's mechanistic emphasis is on chemotherapy-induced stromal disruption. This is a citation relevance issue, not fabrication, but it still constitutes a misleading use of the literature.

2. **Superficial integration of organ-specific biology.** Despite the strong organ-specific framework provided by Dr. Okonkwo, Dr. Bergstrom's translational integration largely ignores organ-specific differences in perioperative risk. The surgical vulnerability window is unlikely to be uniform across bone marrow, lung, liver, and brain metastatic sites, yet the briefing treats it as monolithic.

3. **Missing: pharmacokinetic and pharmacodynamic considerations.** If macrophage-reprogramming agents are to be used perioperatively, their tissue distribution, half-life, and timing relative to surgery are critical. The briefing is silent on these practical considerations.

4. **Insufficient attention to immune checkpoint interactions.** If macrophage polarization is being therapeutically manipulated, the interaction with checkpoint pathways (PD-1/PD-L1, CTLA-4) must be considered, particularly given the combination approach in MARIO-3.

5. **The bidirectional signaling loops** (TGF-beta amplification, EGF/CSF1 paracrine loops) are mentioned but not formalized into a testable model. A schematic with feedback regulation is needed.

**Grade: C+** -- Necessary translational perspective but insufficiently integrated with the organ-specific and mechanistic data from other team members. Citation relevance issue with Ganesan et al. 2023.

### 2.7 Prof. Hannah Klein -- Senior Scientist, Therapeutic Synthesis

**Strengths:**
- The eganelisib pathway is well-positioned as the lead therapeutic candidate, with appropriate integration of MARIO-1 and MARIO-3 trial data.
- The CSF-1R paradox is correctly highlighted as a cautionary principle: depleting all TAMs is not equivalent to reprogramming them.
- The prioritization of PI3K-gamma inhibition over CSF-1R depletion is therapeutically sound and well-justified.

**Critical Deficiencies:**

1. **Insufficient engagement with the organ-specific framework.** Prof. Klein's therapeutic synthesis largely ignores the organ-specific differences in macrophage biology that Dr. Okonkwo's briefing established. PI3K-gamma inhibition may be effective in reprogramming bone marrow macrophages but ineffective or counterproductive in the lung, where alveolar macrophage TGF-betaRIII signaling (Dalla et al. 2024) maintains dormancy through a PI3K-gamma-independent mechanism. This organ specificity of therapeutic response is a critical gap.

2. **Over-reliance on Phase I/Ib data.** The MARIO-1 and MARIO-3 trials provide safety data and preliminary efficacy signals, but neither was designed to test dormancy maintenance. Prof. Klein extrapolates from these trials to the dormancy context without adequate caveats. A Phase II trial specifically designed to test dormancy endpoints (circulating tumor cell dynamics, time to metastatic recurrence) would be needed, and this should have been explicitly proposed.

3. **Missing: combination strategy analysis.** The synthesis does not adequately explore rational combinations. If PI3K-gamma inhibition reprograms macrophages and checkpoint blockade enhances T cell surveillance, the combination may be synergistic for dormancy maintenance. Alternatively, combining PI3K-gamma inhibition with agents that stabilize the collagen III scaffold (promoting DDR1/STAT1 signaling in DTCs) could address both the macrophage and niche components simultaneously. These possibilities are unexplored.

4. **The HDAC2-SP1 epigenetic axis** is mentioned in Prof. Klein's synthesis but without adequate mechanistic integration. If HDAC inhibitors can reprogram macrophage polarization toward a dormancy-promoting phenotype, this represents a parallel therapeutic strategy to PI3K-gamma inhibition. The relative merits, potential synergies, and risks of combining these approaches should have been analyzed.

5. **Missing: biomarker strategy.** If we are to therapeutically manipulate macrophage polarization for dormancy maintenance, we need biomarkers to identify patients who would benefit. Prof. Klein does not address this. Candidate biomarkers include circulating macrophage polarization markers, DTC ERK/p38 ratio (if accessible), and niche imaging biomarkers.

**Grade: B-** -- Sound therapeutic prioritization but insufficiently integrated with the organ-specific biology and mechanistic data. Missing critical elements including biomarker strategy, combination analysis, and clinical trial design recommendations.

Any citation that cannot be verified must be removed and replaced with a verified source, or the supported claim must be removed entirely. This must be completed before any external submission.

## 4. KEY INCONSISTENCIES ACROSS REPORTS

### Inconsistency 1: The M1/M2 Binary Paradox

**The contradiction:** Multiple team members (Drs. Vasquez, Patel; Prof. Moretti) acknowledge that the M1/M2 binary is inadequate and that macrophage polarization exists on a continuum. Yet the same reports, and particularly those of Drs. Chen and Bergstrom, continue to use M1/M2 language as the primary organizing framework for therapeutic strategies.

**Significance:** This is not merely a terminological issue. If macrophage polarization is truly a continuum, then therapeutic strategies aimed at "shifting" macrophages from "M2" to "M1" are conceptually flawed. The appropriate framework is not binary shifting but rather nudging a multidimensional phenotype distribution toward a region of polarization space that favors dormancy maintenance.

**Required action:** The team must adopt a unified terminology based on functional phenotypes (e.g., "dormancy-promoting," "dormancy-permissive," "dormancy-disrupting" macrophages) rather than the M1/M2 labels. Prof. Moretti should lead this terminological reform.

---

### Inconsistency 2: Does PI3K-gamma Inhibition Promote or Disrupt Dormancy?

**The contradiction:** Dr. Vasquez argues that M1-type macrophage signals (TNF-alpha, IL-12, IFN-gamma) promote dormancy via p38 activation. Dr. Chen and Prof. Klein advocate PI3K-gamma inhibition (eganelisib) as the lead therapeutic strategy, arguing that it reprograms M2-like TAMs toward an M1-like phenotype. The implied logic is: PI3K-gamma inhibition --> M2-to-M1 shift --> M1 signals --> p38 activation --> dormancy maintenance.

But Dr. Chen simultaneously identifies the CSF-1R paradox: depleting TAMs can eliminate dormancy-maintaining populations. The same logic should apply to PI3K-gamma inhibition: reprogramming all TAMs toward an M1-like phenotype could eliminate tissue-resident macrophage populations that maintain dormancy through non-inflammatory mechanisms (e.g., alveolar macrophage TGF-betaRIII signaling, as shown by Dalla et al. 2024).

**Significance:** This is the central unresolved question for the therapeutic program. If the team cannot reconcile this contradiction, the entire eganelisib strategy rests on an unexamined assumption.

**Required action:** Dr. Chen and Prof. Klein must produce a detailed analysis of how PI3K-gamma inhibition specifically affects dormancy-promoting macrophage subpopulations in each organ niche. This analysis must incorporate the Dalla et al. 2024 finding that alveolar macrophage TGF-betaRIII signaling maintains dormancy through a mechanism that is not obviously M1 or M2.

---

### Inconsistency 3: The Zheng et al. 2023 Journal Misattribution

**The contradiction:** Multiple team members cited Zheng et al. 2023 as appearing in "Cancer Discovery." Prof. Moretti corrected this to "Cancer Research" (83(14):2345-2357, PMID 37205635). This review has independently verified Prof. Moretti's correction: the paper is indeed in Cancer Research.

**Significance:** If multiple team members independently miscited the same journal, this suggests that they did not consult the primary source but instead copied the citation from each other or from a shared secondary source. This is a form of citation parroting that undermines the integrity of the literature review process.

**Required action:** All team members who cited Zheng et al. 2023 must confirm that they have read the primary source and can describe its key findings in their own words.

---

### Inconsistency 4: Watson et al. 2024 Context Transfer

**The contradiction:** Watson et al. 2024 (confirmed: Cancer Cell 42(9):1507-1527, PMID 39255775) studied macrophage polarization in glioblastoma. It was cited by at least one team member as if its findings were directly applicable to breast cancer DTC dormancy. Glioblastoma and breast cancer DTCs differ fundamentally in tissue context (brain vs. peripheral organs), immune microenvironment (immunoprivileged CNS vs. systemic immune surveillance), and macrophage populations (microglia and bone marrow-derived macrophages vs. tissue-resident macrophages of peripheral organs).

**Significance:** Cross-cancer-type inference is valid when appropriately caveated but misleading when presented as direct evidence. The signaling mechanisms may be conserved, but the microglia-specific context means that therapeutic implications cannot be directly transferred.

**Required action:** Any claims supported by Watson et al. 2024 must be explicitly qualified with "in glioblastoma" and the relevance to breast cancer DTC dormancy must be separately justified.

---

### Inconsistency 5: TGF-beta -- Dormancy Maintainer or Dormancy Escaper?

**The contradiction:** Dr. Vasquez presents TGF-beta1 as a dormancy escape signal through the NRP2 axis. Dalla et al. 2024 (confirmed) presents TGF-beta signaling through TGF-betaRIII on alveolar macrophages as a dormancy maintenance mechanism. Prof. Moretti's synthesis does not resolve this.

**Significance:** TGF-beta is the most context-dependent cytokine in cancer biology. Its role in dormancy is not uniform but depends on receptor expression patterns (TGF-betaRIII vs. NRP2), cellular source (macrophage vs. tumor cell), tissue context (lung vs. bone marrow vs. liver), and temporal dynamics (acute vs. chronic exposure). The team's failure to resolve this duality is a significant analytical gap.

**Required action:** Dr. Vasquez and Prof. Moretti must produce a unified model of TGF-beta signaling in dormancy that accounts for receptor-specific, cell-type-specific, and organ-specific effects.

---

### Inconsistency 6: Surgical Vulnerability Window -- Mechanism Unspecified

**The contradiction:** Dr. Bergstrom identifies a surgical vulnerability window of 1 week to 6 months post-resection. This is an important clinical observation. However, the briefing does not specify which macrophage-related mechanisms drive this window. Is it perioperative M2 polarization (wound healing)? Loss of dormancy-maintaining tissue architecture? Systemic inflammatory cytokine surge? Immunosuppressive anesthesia effects? The window is described but not mechanistically explained.

**Required action:** Dr. Bergstrom must identify the specific macrophage polarization changes during the perioperative period that contribute to dormancy exit, with reference to the organ-specific niches described by Dr. Okonkwo.

## 7. UNIFIED CONCLUSIONS

The following seven conclusions represent the most important takeaways from the team's collective output, as assessed and verified through this critical review.

### Conclusion 1: Macrophage Polarization State Is a Determinant of DTC Dormancy vs. Reactivation

The evidence is strong and convergent: macrophages are not passive bystanders in the DTC niche. Their polarization state directly influences whether DTCs remain quiescent or reactivate to form overt metastases. This is supported by mechanistic data (ERK/p38 ratio modulation via macrophage-derived cytokines), clinical correlative data (macrophage infiltration patterns associated with metastatic timing), and interventional data (macrophage reprogramming alters dormancy outcomes in preclinical models). The M1/M2 binary, while acknowledged as an oversimplification, captures the directional relationship: macrophages producing pro-inflammatory, M1-type signals (TNF-alpha, IL-12, IFN-gamma) tend to promote dormancy maintenance via p38 activation, while macrophages producing anti-inflammatory, M2-type signals (IL-10, TGF-beta) tend to create permissive niches for dormancy exit. However, this binary is insufficient to capture the full complexity of the polarization continuum.

### Conclusion 2: The ERK/p38 Ratio Is the Central Signaling Hub, but Its Heterogeneity Is Underappreciated

The ERK/p38 ratio in DTCs serves as the critical signaling node integrating macrophage-derived signals with tumor-intrinsic dormancy regulation. Low ERK/high p38 = dormancy; high ERK/low p38 = reactivation. However, single-cell analyses reveal that DTC populations exhibit heterogeneous ERK/p38 states, meaning that a subpopulation may be poised for exit even when the bulk population appears dormant. This heterogeneity has critical implications for therapeutic strategies that target the "average" DTC state.

### Conclusion 3: Organ-Specific Niche Biology Is the Key to Translational Progress

The bone marrow, lung, liver, and brain niches employ fundamentally different mechanisms for macrophage-mediated dormancy regulation. The CXCL12/CXCR4 axis dominates in bone marrow; TGF-betaRIII on alveolar macrophages is specific to lung; Kupffer cell/HSC/NK cell interactions characterize the liver; and microglia/laminin-211/YAP signaling defines the brain niche. These organ-specific differences mean that therapeutic strategies cannot be applied uniformly. The Dalla et al. 2024 finding of TGF-betaRIII-mediated dormancy maintenance specifically by alveolar macrophages is a paradigmatic example of why organ-specific analysis matters.

### Conclusion 4: The Type III Collagen / DDR1 / STAT1 Pathway Links ECM Architecture to Dormancy

Type III collagen in the perivascular niche activates DDR1 on DTCs, triggering STAT1-mediated dormancy gene expression. This pathway is significant because it connects the physical microenvironment (ECM composition) to cell-intrinsic dormancy signaling, and it provides a mechanistic explanation for how tissue architecture, not just soluble signals, maintains dormancy. Macrophages can influence this pathway through ECM remodeling (MMP production, collagen deposition).

### Conclusion 5: PI3K-gamma Inhibition (Eganelisib) Is the Lead Therapeutic Candidate but Faces Unresolved Contradictions

Eganelisib is the most clinically advanced candidate for macrophage reprogramming in the dormancy context, with Phase I (MARIO-1) and Phase Ib/II (MARIO-3) data supporting safety and preliminary efficacy. However, the fundamental mechanistic question -- does global PI3K-gamma inhibition promote dormancy maintenance or inadvertently disrupt dormancy-promoting niches? -- remains unresolved. The CSF-1R paradox (TAM depletion triggers fibrosis and may promote dormancy exit) provides a cautionary parallel. A Phase II trial specifically designed with dormancy endpoints is needed.

### Conclusion 6: Bidirectional Tumor-Macrophage Signaling Creates Self-Reinforcing Loops That Must Be Disrupted for Therapeutic Efficacy

DTCs are not passive recipients of macrophage signals. Tumor cells actively educate macrophages through paracrine loops (EGF/CSF1 loop, TGF-beta amplification loop), creating self-reinforcing circuits that stabilize either dormancy-maintaining or dormancy-permissive niches. Therapeutic strategies must account for these feedback loops; disrupting one arm of the loop without addressing the other may lead to therapeutic resistance through circuit reactivation.

### Conclusion 7: Citation Integrity Failures Are a Systemic Problem That Must Be Addressed Before External Submission

Six of 17 audited references (35.3%) are unverifiable in PubMed. This is not a minor editorial matter. It represents a systemic failure of primary-source verification across the team. The He et al. 2026 citation (future date, zero PubMed presence) is particularly concerning. No report containing unverifiable citations should be submitted externally -- to journals, grant agencies, or conference proceedings -- until every citation has been independently verified against PubMed primary sources and all unverifiable references have been removed or replaced.
