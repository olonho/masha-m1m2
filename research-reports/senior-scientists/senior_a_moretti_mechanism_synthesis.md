## I. EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

Three postdoctoral briefings (Drs. Vasquez, Okonkwo, and Bergstrom) have been reviewed and integrated. The reports collectively establish that macrophage polarization is a central rheostat governing disseminated tumor cell (DTC) dormancy entry, maintenance, and escape. The classical M1/M2 binary framework is insufficient; rather, a spectrum of macrophage activation states — defined by ontogeny (tissue-resident vs. monocyte-derived), spatial positioning within organ-specific niches, metabolic programming, epigenetic locking, and exosomal communication — determines DTC fate.

---

## II. INTEGRATED MECHANISTIC FRAMEWORK

### A. Dormancy Induction: Macrophage-Mediated Programming Begins at the Primary Site

Borriello et al. (2022) establishes that a subset of primary tumor-associated macrophages (TAMs), specifically those at TMEM (Tumor Microenvironment of Metastasis) doorways, simultaneously activate both invasion (MenaINV — Menas Involved in Invasion, an isoform of the Mena/ENAH protein) and dormancy (NR2F1 — Nuclear Receptor Subfamily 2 Group F Member 1, SOX9 — SRY-Box Transcription Factor 9) programs in disseminating tumor cells.

### B. The ERK/p38 Signaling Axis as the Intracellular Dormancy Switch

All three briefings converge on the ERK (Extracellular Signal-Regulated Kinase)/p38 ratio as the central intracellular arbiter of dormancy. High p38/low ERK signaling maintains dormancy; a shift toward ERK dominance triggers proliferative outgrowth. NR2F1 acts upstream to reinforce this ratio. Macrophage-derived signals modulate this axis through:

- **TGF-beta2** (Transforming Growth Factor beta 2) from alveolar macrophages (Dalla et al. 2024): sustains dormancy via TGF-betaRIII (TGF-beta Receptor Type III), activating downstream p38/STAT1 (Signal Transducer and Activator of Transcription 1) signaling.
- **IFN-gamma** (Interferon gamma, from CD8+ T cells, with macrophage amplification; Pereira et al. 2025): directly induces dormancy.
- **IL-6** (Interleukin-6)/**G-CSF** (Granulocyte Colony-Stimulating Factor) from stromal injury (Ganesan et al. 2023): activate MEK (MAPK/ERK Kinase)/ERK to break dormancy.

### C. Organ-Specific Niche Architecture

- **Lung:** Alveolar macrophages (tissue-resident, not monocyte-derived) act as a metastasis immune barrier via TGF-beta2. Dalla et al. (2024) proved this using intravital imaging and clodronate/genetic depletion.
- **Bone marrow:** Osteomacs (bone marrow macrophages) and CXCL12 (C-X-C Motif Chemokine Ligand 12)/CXCR4 (C-X-C Chemokine Receptor Type 4) signaling create a permissive niche. M2 macrophage exosomes maintain dormancy via GJIC (Gap Junctional Intercellular Communication; Walker et al. 2019).
- **Liver:** Kupffer cells (liver-resident macrophages) engage the HSC (Hepatic Stellate Cell)-NK (Natural Killer) axis (Correia et al. 2021).
- **Brain:** Microglia (CNS-resident macrophages) and astrocytic laminin-211/YAP (Yes-Associated Protein) signaling modulate dormancy — least characterized niche.

### D. ECM as a Physical Dormancy Scaffold

Di Martino et al. (2022) demonstrated that tumor-derived Type III collagen constructs a pro-dormancy ECM (Extracellular Matrix) niche, with DDR1 (Discoidin Domain Receptor 1)/STAT1 signaling forming a positive feedback loop. Macrophage-derived MMPs (Matrix Metalloproteinases) degrade this collagen to enable escape.

### E. Bidirectional Feedback Loops

1. **TGF-beta amplification:** Macrophage-derived TGF-beta induces DTC dormancy, which feeds back to maintain an immunosuppressive niche favoring M2-like macrophage persistence.
2. **EGF (Epidermal Growth Factor)/CSF-1 (Colony Stimulating Factor 1) paracrine loop:** DTCs secrete CSF-1 to recruit and polarize macrophages, which reciprocate with EGF, reinforcing protumor signaling.

### F. Epigenetic Locking of Macrophage Polarization

Zheng et al. (2023) demonstrated that the HDAC2 (Histone Deacetylase 2)-SP1 (Specificity Protein 1) axis locks macrophages into an M2-like protumor state via histone H3 acetylation and SP1 transcriptional control. DNMT3a (DNA Methyltransferase 3 Alpha)-mediated DNA methylation provides additional stabilization of the M2 state.

### G. Metabolic Reprogramming

Metabolic switches (glycolysis/OXPHOS — Glycolysis/Oxidative Phosphorylation, itaconate, succinate, arginine metabolism via iNOS — Inducible Nitric Oxide Synthase vs. ARG1 — Arginase 1) integrate with hypoxia-driven M2 polarization via HIF-1alpha (Hypoxia-Inducible Factor 1-alpha).

## III. CONVERGENT THEMES

1. **The M1/M2 binary is obsolete.** All postdocs agree that a spectrum model (TREM2+ — Triggering Receptor Expressed on Myeloid cells 2, SPP1+ — Secreted Phosphoprotein 1/Osteopontin, FOLR2+ — Folate Receptor Beta TAMs; resident vs. monocyte-derived) better captures macrophage diversity.

2. **TGF-beta is the single most important dormancy cytokine.** Appears across all briefings.

3. **Chemotherapy can paradoxically awaken dormant DTCs.** Ganesan et al. (2023): taxane-induced stromal injury → IL-6/G-CSF → MEK/ERK activation.

4. **Macrophage exosomes are functional dormancy regulators.** M2 exosomes maintain dormancy via GJIC; M1 exosomes break dormancy via NF-kappaB (Nuclear Factor kappa-light-chain-enhancer of activated B cells).

---

## IV. KEY CONTRADICTIONS AND TENSIONS

1. **Is dormancy T-cell dependent or macrophage dependent?** Pereira et al. (2025) show T-cell independence for maintenance; Dalla et al. (2024) show macrophage dependence. Complementary rather than contradictory — different cell types govern induction vs. maintenance.

2. **TGF-beta as both tumor suppressor and promoter.** Context-dependent: direct TGF-betaRIII signaling induces cytostatic dormancy; TGF-beta-driven stromal remodeling creates protective niches.

3. **CSF-1R (Colony Stimulating Factor 1 Receptor) inhibition: promise or liability?** Watson et al. (2024): fibrotic response to macrophage depletion creates new pro-dormancy niches.

## VII. IDENTIFIED GAPS

1. **Brain niche dormancy** — no primary research demonstrating macrophage-mediated dormancy regulation in brain metastasis
2. **Temporal dynamics** of macrophage repolarization in vivo during DTC awakening — untested
3. **scRNA-seq resolution** of dormant niche macrophages — no study has profiled macrophages in direct contact with dormant vs. proliferative DTCs
4. **DNMT3a epigenetic locking** — claim lacks specific citation
5. **Clinical detection** of dormant DTCs — biomarker gap
6. **Cross-organ comparison** — organ-specific mechanisms not systematically compared
